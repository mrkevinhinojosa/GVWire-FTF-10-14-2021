CORRECTIONAL OFFICER’S BODY ESCORTED FROM L.A. TO FRESNO

The Fresno County Sheriff’s Office honored deceased former correctional officer Toamalama Scanlan with a procession from Los Angeles to Fresno on Wednesday. Scanlan, who was severely injured in the 2016 shooting in the main jail lobby downtown, died Tuesday in a Paramount hospital. He was 46.

HOW WILL FRESNO STATE BULLDOGS DO AGAINST WYOMING?

 

Fresno State’s football team had a bye week to distance memories of its disappointing loss at Hawaii and to prepare for this Saturday’s game against Wyoming. Star quarterback Jake Haener, who got roughed up by Hawaii and had to wear a medical boot for a leg/ankle sprain, is expected to start.

WALTERS: HIGH-SPEED TRAIN PROJECT HITTING THE BRAKES?

For those who think California government is a Democrat-controlled monolith, the dispute over providing additional funding for the state’s high-speed rail project should dispel that myth. Gov. Gavin Newsom wanted a $4.2 billion appropriation to shore up the much-delayed and increasingly costly project, but Democrats in the Legislature refused to provide it.

HER DREAMS OF BECOMING A U.S. CITIZEN FINALLY CAME TRUE

Maria Garcia had to overcome many fears to obtain her U.S. citizenship more than three decades after leaving her home in Mexico. She credits the San Joaquin College of Law’s New American Legal Clinic with helping her overcome obstacles and reach her goal. Fresno County has the second-lowest rate nationwide of eligible people applying to become naturalized citizens.
